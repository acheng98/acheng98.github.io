About

I’m Anthony Cheng, a recent graduate of MIT, where I majored in Materials Science and Engineering (with a focus on Entrepreneurship and Materials Commercialization), with a double minor in Computer Science and Energy Studies. In MIT-speak, that’s 3A-15 with a double minor in 6-3 and ESM.

I’m passionate about solving humanity’s big problems, particularly in electric power systems and energy storage. While most of my work experiences have involved data science and visualization, I believe I’ll make an impact in this world by combining engineering and business chops (“technoeconomics”) to solve our sustainability challenges. 

I care a lot about helping others, both on a “micro” community level and a “macro” global world problems level. Two things I’m proud of doing at MIT are my involvement in StartLabs (MIT’s undergrad entrepreneurship club) and MacGregor House (my living community). In 2019, I acted as an advisor of StartLabs and President of MacGregor, after serving as a co-director and entry chair of MacGregor’s H Entry, respectively, in 2018. In both roles, I built up the organization by establishing culture through memorable experiences (Entrepalooza, Tech Trek to Israel, IKEA trip, Creative Cooking). 

In the past, I’ve been selected as one of the nation’s top high school STEM students, presented at International Science Fairs, represented the United States at the National Geographic World Championships, performed as a piano soloist with the Utah Symphony and at locations like Carnegie Hall, and been recognized with a number of awards.

In the near future, I’ll be doing research into Decarbonization of the Industrial Sector under the Eloranta Fellowship this summer 2020, producing longform audio content about deep technology innovation. In summer 2021, I hope to be able to finish my goal of biking and teaching across the United States with MIT Spokes, which was postponed from summer 2020. I aim to join an interdisciplinary energy PhD program beginning fall 2021, doing research into the technoeconomics of decarbonization. 

I also enjoy reading, creative media, cooking, blogging about the NBA, beatboxing/a cappella, and other elements of the arts.
